The seven chakras, read through the nervous system

A calm reference that keeps the useful structure of the chakra map while separating what is traditional correspondence from what is measurable biology. Endocrine glands are deliberately left out — that mapping is the least defensible part and shifts between sources.

The chakra system is a spiritual map, not an anatomical one, and there is no scientific evidence that chakras exist as physical structures. What is real: the nerve plexuses below are genuine anatomy, and yoga measurably shifts the nervous system — practice can raise heart-rate variability and vagal tone and lower cortisol. Wherever a claim rests on actual evidence it is flagged as a real hook.

Why the glands were removed

The chakra-to-gland mapping is the weakest link in this map — the assigned gland changes between sources (root is "adrenals" in one, "gonads" in another; third-eye and crown swap pituitary and pineal). The nerve plexuses are far better anchored in real anatomy, so this version keeps those and drops the rest.

How much to trust the evidence

The yoga research is real but often from small, lower-quality studies. Trust the direction — practice tends to move the body toward a calmer autonomic state — more than any exact magnitude. Nothing here verifies that a chakra causes these effects; only that the anatomy and the effects themselves are real.
